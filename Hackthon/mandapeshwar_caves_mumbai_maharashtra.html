Mandapeshwar Caves

About the Mandapeshwar Caves

The Mandapeshwar Caves (Marathi: मंडपेश्वर गुहा) are 8th-century rock-cut shrines dedicated to Shiva, located near Mount Poinsur in Borivali, a suburb of Mumbai. Initially, they were occupied by Buddhist monks and reflect the art of the late Gupta Empire.

Key Information:

  • Located in Borivali, northern suburb of Mumbai
  • Part of four rock-cut temples in Mumbai: Elephanta, Jogeshwari, Mahakali, and Mandapeshwar Caves
  • Sculptures include Nataraja, Sadashiva, Ardhanarishvara, Ganesha, Brahma, and Vishnu
  • Depicts mythical tales of Hindu gods and goddesses
  • Features an elaborate sculpture representing the marriage of Shiva with Parvati
  • Declared as an archaeological heritage site, protected under law
