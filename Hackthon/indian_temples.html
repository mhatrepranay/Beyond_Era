Indian Temples

About Indian Temples

Indian temples, dating back to the early centuries, showcase the country's rich spiritual and architectural history. Temples started as rock-cut structures and evolved into intricate forms influenced by dynasties like the Guptas, Cholas, Hoysalas, and Vijayanagara rulers. These temples served as centers for worship, art, education, and community life. Today, they remain as cultural icons, preserved as heritage sites and vibrant places of worship.

Architectural Styles

Indian temples are mainly categorized into three architectural styles:

  • Nagara (North India): Features tall, curvilinear spires, seen in Khajuraho and Konark.
  • Dravida (South India): Known for large gopurams (gateways) and pyramid-like towers, as in Brihadeeswara and Meenakshi temples.
